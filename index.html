Accounts of life in Abudi

The sustained drought in Abudi has created widespread food insecurity.

The setting for this topic is a rural village in the north of the country. The Abudians we meet are people who have stayed in their homes rather than migrating to the temporary settlements.

Revise the description of “non-displaced people” in Shelter and Settlement Appendix 2: Description of settlement scenarios.

Read her story and consider whether her human rights are being met.

The crops are failing and our animals are dying. It seems to get worse each year. The price of food is so high now that we do not have enough money to buy what we need at market. It is hard to find water. The wells are dry, so we collect what we can from shallow pools. We know the water is unclean and is responsible for the rise in sickness and diarrhoea, but the clean water sources are too far away.
